Race and Empire

Bristol has played a particularly central role within the history of British Empire and racism from Bristol's growth and enrichment fueled by the Trans-Atlantic slave trade, to the Bristol Bus Boycott and the St Paul's riots. Racism continues today - as evidenced through police harassment, anti-immigrant attitudes and legislation, the Windrush deportations, Islamophobia, Anti-Semitism and the rise of fascism - and we are still discussing the status of Winston Churchill in history and removing slave-trader Colston's name in Bristol. Our panel will discuss the consequences of Empire and explore ideas of how we dismantle the structural racism found in our institutions and communities.

Panel: Edson Burton, Ras Judah, Madge Dresser, Sasha Josette, Dalia Gebrial

Municipal Socialism

In recent years many have been calling the prevailing Neoliberal order of politics and economics into question. We were told by the people in charge that this was the most efficient and equitable way to organise a successful society and economy, but we are surrounded by evidence that this isn't so and people are increasingly challenging this narrative. This panel will explore alternatives based on local democratic control, worker owned cooperatives and decommodification.

Chair: Hettie O'Brien, Panel: Dr Julian Manley, Cat Hobbs (We Own It), Frankie Langeland, more TBC!

Social Media Workshop

Like it or not, social media is playing an increasingly prevalent role in politics. Be it in reaching new audiences, in increasing our ability to engage directly with politicians or in determining the political narrative. Social media can be a powerful force for the left, if used well.

Laura Ho is a Digital Community Organiser for the Labour Party, she works with grassroots campaigners across the South West on local issue-based campaigns. She scales the campaigns, builds campaign narrative and tells the stories of people effected by Tory policy through online platforms.

Come to this workshop to learn some easy tips to drastically improve your social media game

Putting Class back into Intersectionality

This interactive workshop explores the concepts of Marxism, Identity Politics and Intersectionality, before looking at a case study of union organising among an ethnically diverse workforce. We consider the different opinions as to whether workplace issues were racialised and the extent to which a common identity is a mobilising opportunity.

We don't claim to have all the answers, and while it is possible that there will be many different conclusions, at the end of the workshop, we will at least be asking relevant questions about how we build unity, and solidarity in the modern era.

Resisting Surveillance Capitalism

With the digitalization of the economy we are confronted with a new information order often described as ‘surveillance capitalism’ that thrives on the exploitation of huge amounts of data across our social and everyday lives. How should we make sense of these developments? And what do they mean for long-standing struggles for social, economic, racial and environmental justice? In this talk we’ll take stock of some of the ways data is collected and used in society, their implications, and how we might advance alternative data infrastructures and economies.
With Lina Dencik (Data Justice Lab, Cardiff University
